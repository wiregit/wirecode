LimeWire Translation Contributors

This is a small list of supported locales for LimeWire, with their contributors, and their area of work.

LimeWire would like to address special thanks to all the following contributors that have helped localizing the application for their linguistic community. Mainly, supporting a locale is just a matter of translating appropriate resources used by the LimeWire application and its distribution website.

This list is maintained separately in order to remove contact information in distributed localized files when this is requested by the sender. The email contact addresses are shown with " DOT " and " AT " replacing the actual '.' and '@' characters, in order to limit successful parsing this document by spamwares and viral robots, to help protect the privacy of our contributors; restore these characters manually in your email agent when sending messages:

  • If the contributor is registered, then the email address in the limewire.org domain should be preferably used. Search for it in the online Members list.
  • If the contribution was purposely anonymous, this list should not contain the contact information. And a registered user may review it and take the lead on it.

There may be multiple area of work in the future, including new input methods and specific resource bundles:

  • The "Messages" work area designates the translation of the general "MessagesBundle.properties" used by the LimeWire application.
  • The "download.html" work area is the translation of the main download page on the limewire.com website.
  • The "XML-metadata labels" work area is the translation of the input labels displayed in the advanced search panel, when a media category is selected.
  • The "about.html" work area is the translation of the HTML page displayed in the About box of the LimeWire application (in the Help menu).
